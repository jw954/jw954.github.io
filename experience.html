Research Experience

Computer Vision Research Intern — Toronto Computational Imaging Group

Aug 2025 – Present · Toronto, ON, Canada

Working on undergraduate thesis focused on generating color images/videos/bursts from binary quanta frames using single-photon imaging at 100,000 fps. Utilizing real data captures from single photon avalanche diodes (SPAD) and examining distribution of generations and performance in low-light conditions where conventional CMOS cameras fail. Calibrating SPAD dataset to implement a non-parametric single photon simulation pipeline from standard RGB datasets. Performing downstream tasks such as segmentation and detection solely from one-bit images based generations.

Computer Vision Research Intern — Safe AI Lab, Carnegie Mellon University

Aug 2024 – Present · Pittsburgh, PA, USA · Supervisor: Prof. Ding Zhao

Co-first author of FeatureEndo-4DGS: Real-Time Deformable Surgical Scene Reconstruction and Segmentation with 4D Gaussian Splatting (ML4H Symposium 2025, PMLR). Innovated the first real-time 4D Gaussian Splatting pipeline that jointly reconstructs and segments deformable surgical scenes via feature distillation from 2D foundation models. Leveraged foundation models for semantic scene understanding and outperformed SOTA methods (LGS, EndoGaussian, EndoNeRF) on PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, and RMSE. Achieved competitive segmentation vs. SAM/SAM2/MedSAM with higher Dice and IoU. Ablations showed a lightweight semantic feature decoder boosts segmentation scores by up to 10%. Worked with CUDA and C++ to adapt parallelized rasterizers for training and rendering. Explored autoregressive large models with diffusion-loss prediction of Gaussians and camera properties for rigid objects.

Computer Vision Research Intern — Intelligent Sensory Microsystems Laboratory (ISML)

Apr 2023 – Sep 2023 · Toronto, ON, Canada · Supervisor: Prof. Roman Genov

Contributed to work published at IEEE VLSI 2024 on software-defined image sensors. Automated fundamental matrix calculation for pixel-coded epipolar imaging with OpenCV (achieving >600% speed-up). Adapted RAFT-inspired deep learning for optical flow using flux over intensity-based methods. Devised frame-differencing for color-coded flow maps and motion estimation. Generated banded mask patterns to filter indirectly/directly lit light. Implemented Huffman, RLE, and Wavelet transforms for optimized lossless compression.
